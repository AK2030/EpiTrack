Coronavirus
COVID-19 is a common viral infection that has infected more than 6.79 million people in the US within the last year. It is caused by a strain of the coronavirus, and causes severe respiratory illness. If you suspect you or a loved one has coronavirus, make sure to get tested at testing centersnear you and seek medical attention if it gets severe.

Coronavirus (Covid-19)

  • Symptons (range from mild to severe, if one has symptoms at all)
    • Fever
    • Cough
    • Tiredness
    • A loss of taste or smell
    • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
    • Muscle aches
    • Chills
    • Sore throat
    • Runny nose
    • Headache
    • Chest pain
    • Pink eye (conjunctivitis)
    • Rash
    • Nausea and vomiting
    • Diarrhea
  • It is important to realize that many people are asymptomatic and may still be spreading the disease without knowing that they are doing so
    •
  • Causes
    • Infection with the new coronavirus, also called SARS-CoV-2
    • The virus spreads by droplets released when someone with the virus sneezes, coughs, or talks
      • The droplets are either inhaled or land in the mouth or nose of a nearby person
    • It can spread by touching a surface and then touching one’s mouth, nose, or eyes, but this isn’t a main method of spread
  • Populations at risk
    • Middle-aged and older adults
      • People of any age can get the disease, but the risk of developing dangerous symptoms increases with age
    • Those with lung problems
      • COPD
      • Lung cancer
      • Cystic fibrosis
      • Pulmonary fibrosis
      • Moderate to severe asthma
    • Those with other chronic conditions, especially those that weaken the immune system
      • Heart disease
      • Diabetes
      • Obesity
    • Those with weakened immune systems
      • Organ transplant recipients
      • Those with cancer
      • Those with HIV/AIDS
      • Those with long-term use of prednisone and other immunosuppressant drugs
    • Those with chronic kidney or liver disease
      • Those on dialysis are especially vulnerable
  • Prevention
    • Avoid large events and mass gatherings
      • Remember that COVID spreads through close contact with other people because droplets can travel up to 6 feet from an infected person’s nose or mouth
      • In a situation where you are close to other people, you are likely to get infected
    • Socially distance by staying 6 feet apart from other people
      • Asymptomatic people can spread the disease without you realizing that they have it
      • Staying 6 feet apart will prevent you from accidentally catching the virus
    • Stay home as much as possible
    • Wash hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds
      • Use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol if soap and water is not available
    • Wear a mask in public spaces where it is difficult to avoid close contact with others
      • Though the issue of wearing a mask has become extremely politicized, the fact remains that the mask acts as a barrier to prevent droplets carrying the virus from entering your nose and mouth
      • Wearing a mask can also prevent you from spreading the disease if you are asymptomatic
      • Make sure the mask you are using is a nonmedical cloth mask, not an N95 respirator or a surgical mask, as those are reserved for health care providers
    • Cover your nose and mouth with your elbow or a tissue when you cough or sneeze
      • Throw the tissue away immediately after and wash your hands
    • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, or mouth
    • Avoid sharing dishes, glasses, towels, bedding, and other household items if you are sick
    • Clean and disinfect high-touch surfaces like doorknobs, light switches, electronics, and counters every day
      • This is to prevent surface transmission
